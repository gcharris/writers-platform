### **Part 2: The Exposition – The Facility Tour & Briefing**

**Objective:** To provide the "official" explanation of the technology to Mickey and Noni. Each scientist delivers a piece of the puzzle, but the complete picture is deliberately misleading. The structure should be: Problem (Ward) -> Hardware Solution (Surgery/Q5) -> Environment (Big Brain) -> The Secret Sauce (Briefing).

**A. The Tour (Show, Don't Tell)**

1. **The Ward:** This scene establishes the **problem**. Richard must explain that the dying bodies are a result of **"desynchronization"**—the mind drifting from a body that is no longer receiving commands, causing it to "forget" how to live.
2. **Faulk's Surgery:** This scene introduces the **hardware solution**. Richard shows them the **Q5 implant** and explains that its purpose is to create a powerful **"synchronous feedback loop"** that **"anchors the consciousness to the physical form,"** preventing the decay they saw in the ward.
3. **The "Big Brain" Room:** This introduces the **destination**. Richard introduces the quantum computer and defers to **Dr. Liang** as the expert. Liang should explain that a **quantum computer** is the only architecture capable of creating a true **"environment"** for a consciousness to exist in its native quantum state. This solidifies his role as the QC expert.

This is the formal explanation where the lies of omission are most apparent.

He should highlight the **"deep integration"** of the implant's fibers and the function of his **neural regulators.** Critically, he should also be the one to bring up the **"observer effect,"** framing the Q5's stability as essential to allow for *any* observation of the quantum space without causing the consciousness to "decohere" or collapse. This positions him as a gatekeeper of control and surveillance.

- **3. Igor-Zaitsev (The Theoretical Mastermind / The "Key" Holder):** This is the climax of the exposition. After the "what" has been explained, Igor explains the secret sauce, the *real* reason the UNRCAC's work is so far ahead.
